Tourism Support & FAQs

Everything you need to know for your Egyptian adventure.

Our goal is to ensure every visitor experiences the magic of Egypt with confidence. From navigating the bustling streets of Cairo to exploring the tranquil temples of Upper Egypt, this FAQ addresses common concerns to help you plan a seamless trip.

Do I need a visa to enter Egypt?
Most nationalities require a visa. You can obtain an e-Visa online via the official portal (visa2egypt.gov.eg) or purchase a 30-day tourist visa on arrival at major airports for $25 USD (cash).
When is the best time to visit? Recommended
The ideal window is October to April. Temperatures are mild (20°C–28°C), making it perfect for sightseeing. If you visit in summer (June-August), expect intense heat, but enjoy lower prices and fewer crowds at the Red Sea resorts.
Is it safe to drink the tap water?
No. It is highly recommended to drink bottled or filtered water only. Bottled water is inexpensive and available everywhere. Even for brushing teeth, many travelers prefer using bottled water.
What is the currency and can I use credit cards?
The local currency is the Egyptian Pound (EGP). While hotels and large malls accept credit cards, cash is essential for local markets (souks), tipping, and smaller cafes. ATMs are widely available in cities.
What should I wear while sightseeing?
Egypt is conservative. While resorts are relaxed, when visiting cities or religious sites, it's respectful to cover your shoulders and knees. Light, breathable cotton fabrics are best for the desert sun.
