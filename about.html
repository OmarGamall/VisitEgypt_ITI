Discover Egypt: Where Time Began

Egypt is a land where thousands of years of history live alongside a vibrant modern culture. Known as the "Gift of the Nile," it is a transcontinental country spanning the northeast corner of Africa and the southwest corner of Asia, making it a unique bridge between worlds.

7,000 Years of History

From the Great Pyramids of the Old Kingdom to the Greco-Roman wonders of Alexandria and the Islamic architecture of Old Cairo, Egypt is a living museum of human progress and engineering.

The Mighty Nile

The Nile is the heartbeat of the nation. Most of Egypt's cities lie along its banks, creating a lush green valley surrounded by vast golden deserts and framed by the Mediterranean and Red Seas.

Warm Hospitality

Egyptians are world-famous for their humor and "Karram" (hospitality). Whether you are exploring a busy bazaar or a quiet village, you will find a warm welcome and a cup of tea waiting for you.

Quick Facts for Visitors

  • Capital: Cairo
  • Language: Arabic (Egyptian Dialect)
  • Currency: Egyptian Pound (EGP)
  • Best Time to Visit: October to April (Cooler weather)
  • Top Cities: Cairo, Alexandria, Luxor, Aswan, and Hurghada
